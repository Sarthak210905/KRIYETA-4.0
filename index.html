Vision

To create a sustainable ecosystem of innovation and collaboration that empowers individuals to leverage technology for societal impact. Kriyeta fosters creativity and technical excellence, enabling problem-solvers to develop real-world solutions that address humanitarian and technological challenges.

Mission

Empower individuals with the technical expertise and ethical awareness to develop impactful solutions for real-world problems, including those faced by underserved communities.

Encourage youth to embrace a problem-solving mindset through practical innovation, fostering projects that align with global humanitarian needs.

Promote knowledge exchange, rapid prototyping, and interdisciplinary collaboration to drive technological advancements that contribute to disaster recovery, sustainable development, and societal well-being.

About KRIYETA 4.0

We are thrilled to present KRIYETA 4.0, a prestigious 48-hour hackathon, dedicated to fostering innovation and creativity among tech enthusiasts. The event will be hosted at the esteemed Acropolis Institute of Technology and Research, Indore, providing an ideal platform for students and professionals to showcase their talents in diverse domains like Sustainable Technologies, Healthcare Innovation, AI and Emerging Technologies, Agriculture and Food Security.

This platform promotes creativity and technology to solve humanitarian issues. We want to become a humanitarian technology hub by training, educating, and problem-solving participants to build relevant solutions. This event uses best practices and progress tracking to ensure sustainable and demonstrable impact. We raise awareness of engineering's social effect through focused outreach, partner collaborations, and thought leadership by highlighting IEEE's position in technology for social good. Strategic relationships with business, academia, and worldwide organizations help us scale breakthrough solutions and foster technology-driven transformation. Help us shape humanitarian engineering via invention, collaboration, and action.

Glimpses

KRIYETA 3.0

    KRIYETA 3.0, a 48-hour national hackathon, was the third edition of the successful Kriyeta initiative by the Department of CSE (AI&ML) and the Entrepreneurship Development Cell of Acropolis Institute of Technology and Research, sponsored by the Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR). The event aimed to foster innovation and empower technologists to tackle real-world challenges in domains like Blockchain, Web Development, Machine Learning, AI, and Mobile App Development


    The hackathon attracted teams from states like Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Karnataka, and Madhya Pradesh, including students from IITs and NITs. From 100-120 registered teams, 57 were shortlisted for an online presentation round on Unstop, and 31 advanced to the on-site Round-2 held from 14th to 16th June 2024. This phase brought together diverse perspectives and expertise.


    Meticulously organized by faculty and student volunteers, the event ensured participants' comfort with wellarranged amenities like food, medical facilities, and recreational spaces. The goal was to nurture talent, stimulate creativity, and foster collaboration, contributing to technological advancement and a problem-solving mindset in India.

KRIYETA 2.0

KRIYETA 2.0, a 48-hour national hackathon, was a sequel to the highly successful Kriyeta initiative by the Institution's Innovation Council (IIC), in collaboration with AICTE Idea Lab AITR, the Department of CSE (AI&ML), and the Entrepreneurship Development Cell of Acropolis Institute of Technology and Research. The event aimed to foster innovation and empower technologists to tackle real-world challenges in domains like Smart Manufacturing, Data Science, Web Development, IoT, AI, and Mobile App Development.


The hackathon attracted teams from across the country, including Uttar Pradesh, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and Madhya Pradesh. Out of 100-120 registered teams, 45 were shortlisted, showcasing diverse perspectives and expertise. Meticulously organized by faculty and student volunteers, the event ensured participants' comfort with well-arranged amenities, including food, medical facilities, and recreational spaces.


The goal was to nurture talent, stimulate creativity, and foster collaboration, contributing to the advancement of technology and a problem-solving mindset in India.

KRIYETA 1.0

    Kriyeta 1.0 achieved a remarkable feat by uniting 200 participants from diverse corners of India, including Andhra Pradesh, Ghaziabad, Bhopal, and Gwalior, reflecting its widespread impact. With a substantial 50k prize pool, the event not only rewarded excellence but also provided a platform for creative minds to collaborate, conceive pioneering solutions, and exchange ideas for current challenges. The event's commitment to interdisciplinary cooperation enabled participants to realize their potential and bring revolutionary ideas to life.


    Kriyeta 1.0's influence stretched beyond physical boundaries, exemplified by complete live streaming on YouTube, underscoring its transparency and inclusivity. Rules

  • Participants must bring their own systems for the hackathon. The organizing team will only provide internet connectivity. It is the responsibility of each team to ensure they have the necessary hardware and software tools to participate effectively.

  • Each team should choose a designated color to represent their identity. All team members are required to wear the chosen color during the event for easy identification

  • Throughout the entire duration of the event, a minimum of 50 percent of the team members must be present at the designated arena. Failure to meet this requirement may result in disqualification

  • Each team must consist of 4 to 5 members, ensuring a collaborative environment. It is mandatory to have at least one female participant in each team, promoting diversity and inclusivity

  • Participants are required to remain on the premises for the entire duration of the 48-hour hackathon. Leaving the premises during the event is strictly prohibited

  • Any participant found engaging in unethical practices, including but not limited to cheating, plagiarism, or unauthorized use of resources, will lead to disqualification of the entire team. Upholding integrity and ethical conduct is paramount in maintaining a fair and competitive environment

    • NOTE: These rules and regulations are in place to ensure a fair, inclusive, and professional hackathon experience for all participants. Violation of any of these rules may result in penalties or disqualification, as determined by the organizing committee.
